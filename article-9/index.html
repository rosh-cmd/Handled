How to Get Consistent Listing Marketing Without Hiring a Full-Time Designer

Hiring a full-time designer sounds like the obvious solution when your listing marketing is inconsistent. One person, dedicated to your agency, who knows your brand and produces everything you need.

The reality is more complicated. A junior designer costs $55,000–$70,000 per year in Melbourne once you factor in super, leave, and onboarding. They need managing. They get sick. They leave — usually at the worst possible time — and take the institutional knowledge with them. And for many agencies, the volume of design work isn't consistent enough to justify a full-time salary every week of the year.

Handled Studio works with Melbourne real estate agencies on ongoing listing collateral, social, and proposals — without the cost or complexity of a full-time hire.

There's a version of getting consistent listing marketing that doesn't involve hiring. Here's what it actually looks like.

Why agencies default to hiring anyway

The instinct makes sense. If design is a persistent problem — inconsistent output, too much of the principal's time spent reviewing, listings going out looking different every week — the obvious fix is to bring someone in-house and solve it permanently.

But in-house hiring solves the availability problem, not the quality problem. A junior designer who's available 38 hours a week still needs clear briefs, locked templates, a managed asset library, and someone senior enough to set the standard and catch what's not right before it goes out.

Most agencies don't have that infrastructure in place. So they hire someone, spend six months getting them up to speed, and find themselves in exactly the same situation — just with more payroll.

The actual requirement

What real estate agencies need from design is not a person. It's a system.

A system that produces listing kits to a consistent standard every time. A system where templates are locked and up to date. A system where a new listing brief takes 20 minutes, not 90. A system where someone senior is looking at everything before it goes out — not because they've been asked to, but because that's the job.

That system can be built without a hire. But it requires the right kind of external partner — not a freelancer who turns up when called, and not an agency that treats every job as a new project.

What this looks like month to month

A well-structured retainer for a mid-sized Melbourne agency typically covers:

  • 6–12 listing kits per month (brochure, flyer, window card per property)
  • 12–20 social assets across the agency's channels
  • 1–2 EDMs or campaign tiles
  • Ongoing template maintenance and asset library updates
  • Sold and just-listed announcements as they occur

Everything produced to a single standard, by someone who knows the brand, on turnarounds that work for real estate.

The cost sits between what a part-time coordinator costs and what a full-time junior designer costs — with none of the HR overhead and a quality level that a junior hire can't match.

The transition question

If you're currently producing listing marketing through a mix of admin staff, Canva, and the occasional freelancer, the question isn't whether a retainer would be better — it almost certainly would be. The question is whether the output you're getting now is costing you more than you realise.

Inconsistent listing marketing affects vendor perception, agent confidence, and the agency's reputation in the market. Those aren't costs that show up on a P&L, but they're real.
