Kria Mortgage Managers

A brand that finally matches the business

A five-year partnership with Kria Mortgage Managers — from their original identity to a full rebrand, launch assets, and an ongoing template system built to match an award-winning, repositioned business.

5+ Years
Rebrand + Templates
Returning Client

The Context

Kria Mortgage Managers is an award-winning Melbourne mortgage management business. I first designed their brand five years ago, when they were an earlier-stage operation finding their position in the market.

In the years since, Kria has done what strong businesses do. They repositioned, sharpened what they stood for, and won a series of industry awards along the way — becoming a far more established and credible operation than the one their original brand was built for.

The brand hadn't kept pace. It still worked, but it no longer looked like the business Kria had become. For a referral-and-trust business, where every client and partner forms an impression from what they can see, that gap was worth closing properly.

The Approach

Rather than a one-off logo refresh, the work was built as a complete brand system — designed to reflect the repositioned business and to hold together as Kria continues to grow.

It began the way the original engagement did: with the right questions. Understanding what the brand needed to stand for now, not just what it should look like. From there, the work spanned a full rebrand, the launch assets to bring it to market cleanly, and a template library covering the materials Kria produces regularly.

The relationship continues today. I work with Kria as their ongoing design partner — maintaining the system and producing new work within it — so the brand stays consistent across every client touchpoint, regardless of who is producing what or when.

The Outcome

A brand that matches the award-winning business behind it — and a system built to keep it that way.

Kria now has a consistent, on-brand presence across every client-facing and marketing touchpoint, produced from one maintained set of templates rather than assembled piece by piece. New work slots into an established system instead of starting from scratch each time.

The result is a brand that looks the part today and is built to keep looking the part as the business grows — supported by a design partnership that has now continued across five years and two stages of the company's life.
