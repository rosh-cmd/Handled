Questions people ask before they book

We take on your ongoing design production. The output your business generates week to week. For real estate agencies that means listing presentations, appraisal kits, social tiles, EDMs, signage, proposals, and campaign collateral. For construction businesses it's capability statements, tender documents, team bios, and project photography integration. You brief us on what needs doing, we produce it to your brand standard, you approve it, it goes out. No rebuilding context each time, no chasing availability and less last-minute fixes.

A freelancer does a project-based job. We own your output. The difference is context — after the first month we know your brand the way an in-house designer would. We know which logo version to use, what your preferred layout looks like, what's gone out before and what needs to stay consistent with it. A freelancer starts from your new brief every time. We start from everything we already know about your business. That compounds quickly and has proven to be of higher ROI for our clients.

Most businesses we work with are surprised by how much they're actually producing once they add it up — listings, appraisals, social content, proposals, campaigns. The other thing worth considering is the invisible cost: the hours you or your team spend briefing, chasing, reviewing, and fixing. A retainer makes sense when the total overhead of managing your current setup outweighs what a dedicated production relationship would cost. The Fit Check is specifically designed to answer this question. 20 minutes looking at your current output and we'll tell you honestly whether a retainer makes sense for where you are right now.

We use a shared task board called Asana — you add jobs, we pick them up. Each brief comes with a turnaround time based on complexity: most standard production tasks (social tiles, document updates, presentation edits) come back within 24 to 48 hours. Larger jobs are scoped at the start of the month. Each piece goes through one round of revisions as standard — we work towards getting it right the first time because the context is already held. You have direct access throughout. No tickets, no account layers, no waiting for a weekly check-in.

The Handoff — our 30-day pilot — is $1,500 AUD flat, paid at the start of the month. No lock-in, no ongoing commitment. You get real production output, we get to demonstrate what the relationship looks like in practice. If it's working, the ongoing retainer starts from $3,000 AUD per month, scoped to your output volume at the end of the pilot. If it's not, you keep the work and we part ways cleanly.
